# Related Works in UAV-assisted IoT Detection

## 1. UAV-assisted Wireless Sensor Networks (WSN)
UAVs are increasingly used to enhance coverage, connectivity, and data collection in WSNs. Research focuses on mobility models, energy efficiency, and dynamic topology management.

## 2. Fragmented Data Broadcast and Collection
Fragmented broadcast schemes improve reliability and robustness in IoT networks, especially in environments with intermittent connectivity. Studies explore optimal fragment sizes, scheduling, and recovery algorithms.

## 3. Alert Thresholds and Event Detection
Threshold-based detection is a common approach for anomaly or event recognition in distributed sensor networks. Related works analyze adaptive thresholding, false alarm reduction, and multi-node consensus.

## 4. Modular Simulation Architectures in ns-3
Recent research advocates for modular, extensible simulation architectures in ns-3 to facilitate rapid prototyping and reproducibility. This includes reusable classes for nodes, UAVs, channels, and applications.

## 5. Energy Models and Resource Constraints
Energy consumption is a critical factor in WSN and IoT research. Related works propose models for battery usage, energy harvesting, and trade-offs between communication and computation.

## 6. Multi-UAV Coordination and Swarm Intelligence
Multi-UAV systems leverage swarm intelligence for efficient area coverage, data aggregation, and fault tolerance. Key ideas include decentralized control, task allocation, and collaborative sensing.

## 7. Integration of Realistic Physical Layer Models
Accurate physical layer modeling (e.g., CC2420, fading, interference) is essential for realistic simulation results. Related works compare different radio models and their impact on protocol performance.

## 8. Validation and Reproducibility in Simulation Research
Emphasis on trace-driven validation, metrics collection, and reproducibility. Best practices include publishing code, scenarios, and validation scripts alongside research papers.
